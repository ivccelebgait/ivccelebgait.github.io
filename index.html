Abstract

Gait recognition identifies individuals through their walking patterns and body shape, offering advantages such as long-range and non-contact sensing. However, existing gait datasets are mostly collected in controlled environments and rarely reflect the clothing variations and complex scene changes encountered in real-world applications. To facilitate research under more practical settings, we present CelebGait, an in-the-wild clothing-changing gait benchmark containing 4,500 identities and 21,299 sequences, with substantial variations in clothing, scene, viewpoint, and motion dynamics. Extensive evaluations show that the performance of existing gait recognition methods drops notably on CelebGait, highlighting the difficulty of this benchmark. To provide a competitive yet deployment-friendly baseline for CelebGait, we further introduce PoseFreeGait. The proposed baseline exploits both silhouette and pose heatmap modalities during training, while requiring only silhouette inputs at inference time. Specifically, PoseFreeGait first learns a bimodal teacher to capture complementary gait cues, then trains a silhouette-to-pose generator in the latent feature space, and finally performs joint fine-tuning to transfer pose-aware knowledge into a silhouette-driven inference pipeline. In this way, the model can benefit from multimodal supervision during training without relying on pose inputs during testing. Experimental results on CelebGait show that PoseFreeGait provides a strong and practical baseline for clothing-changing gait recognition in unconstrained environments. The dataset and code will be released upon acceptance.
