Abstract

Gait recognition identifies individuals through their walking patterns and body shape, offering advantages such as long-range and non-contact sensing. However, existing gait datasets are mostly collected in controlled environments and rarely reflect the clothing variations and complex scene changes encountered in real-world applications. To facilitate research under more practical settings, we present CelebGait, a large-scale, in-the-wild clothing-changing gait benchmark containing 4,500 identities and 21,299 sequences, with substantial variations in clothing, scene, viewpoint, and motion dynamics. Extensive evaluations show that the performance of existing gait recognition methods drops notably on CelebGait, highlighting the critical bottleneck of clothing interference in unconstrained environments. To tackle the specific challenges introduced by CelebGait, we further establish GaitUniFormer as a tailored multimodal baseline. Instead of treating modalities independently, GaitUniFormer employs a unified transformer-style architecture to achieve fine-grained cross-modal interaction between silhouettes and pose heatmaps. By explicitly anchoring silhouette-based appearance features with stable pose-aware structural dynamics, this baseline effectively mitigates the severe visual interference caused by diverse clothing. Experimental results on CelebGait show that GaitUniFormer provides a robust reference among existing silhouette-based, pose-based, and multimodal approaches, demonstrating the necessity of deep multimodal integration for real-world clothing-changing gait recognition.
